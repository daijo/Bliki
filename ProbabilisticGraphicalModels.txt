Distributions
-------------

Random variables:

Raining(R)
r0(no), r1(yes)
2 parameters

Temperature(T)
t0(cold), t1(medium), t2(hot)
3 parameters

Joint distribution:

P(R, T)

2*3 parameters
5 independent parameters

Conditioning-Reduction:

Conditioning on a parameter p (stating the parameter as given) casue reduction of the joint distribution to a unnormalized measure that can be normalized to a new distribution given p.

Marginalization:

Takes a probability distribution over a larger set of variables and rpoduces a probability distribution over a subset of variables.

Marginalize R means sum P(R, T) over R to produce P(T).

Factors
-------

A factor is a function Val(X1, ... Xk) -> ℝ.

The scope (X1, ... Xk).

For instance a joint distribuion, an unnormalized factor or a conditional probability distribution is a factor.

General factors
---------------

Factor product:

F(A, B) x F(B, C) -> F(A, B ,C)

F(a1, b1, c1) = F(a1, b1) * F(b1, c1) ...
